    Punch Lace Knitting

    Combining thicker yarn and sewing thread for lace-like fabric.

    The technique punch lace requires you to have two working threads at the same time, where one is a yarn of at least fingering/DK weight and the second one a very fine one such as sewing thread. Punch lace primarily works through the creation of the illusion of lace like features in the dominant, thick yarn, while the thread ensures the fabric remains stable and to help tension. The spaces that remain filled on the punch card, indicate the thicker yarn as dominant. The spaces that have been hole-punched create eyelets/gaps in the thicker yarn where the sewing thread shows through. Depending on the thread you use, it may create different illusions or how clearly it is visible, but keep in mind that the sewing thread is knitted alongside the thicker yarn, resulting in the back of the fabric being covered in a layer of the sewing thread - in case you'd like for the back to be visible. A suggestion for the design would be to not create eyelets too large (maximum 2-3 stitches wide), because the dominant yarn will create floats that may be distracting.





    Steps on the machine:
    1. Cast-on thicker yarn and knit at least 7 stockinette rows (add weights!), with your carriage on the left hand side, feed the pattern card into the machine and use the dial to roll it through fully - clip the two ends together.
    2. Thick yarn goes in yarn feeder 1. Thread goes in yarn feeder 2.
    3. Cam lever 'L', left russell lever forwards (in order to hold D position needles), right russell lever flush with carriage.
    4. Before running carriage, pull out both selvedge needles - helps to anchor the selvedges
    4a. NOTE: The selvedge needles need to be reset every other pass of the carriage.
